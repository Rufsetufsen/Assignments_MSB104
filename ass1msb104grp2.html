Data Acquisition

In this assignment we will use four countries data from Eurostat to process it and analyse the sub-national GDP and population data from the years 2000-2023. Eurostat serves as the statistical office of the European Union, and their work is to collect and provide statistics on EU countries, through reliable, impartial and comparable data These countries are Germany, Switzerland, Croatia and Ireland. In these datasets we encountered missing values which we decided to keep. These NA, or missing data came from different reason from each country.

  • Germany which as the most observations We will then calculate the GDP per capita and explore regional inequality.

Regional GDP inequality - Calculating the Gini coefficient

ginigdp <- tidyjoined %>%
  na.omit %>%
  group_by(NUTS2, Time)%>%
  summarise( Count = n(),
            gini = gini.wtd(GDP_Capita, weights = Population))
